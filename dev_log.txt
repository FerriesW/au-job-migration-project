    coverage for ratio stability and is appropriate for a v1 dashboard.

[2026-05-10]  v0.4.0 — LLM extraction pipeline and intermediate enrichment layer
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Authored the adzuna_pipeline.extraction sub-package as five cooperating
    modules:
      * schema.py    — Pydantic model defining the extraction contract
                       (required_skills, years_experience, sponsorship_signal,
                       local_experience_required, remote_friendly) with
                       enum-aliasing pre-validators that normalise common
                       LLM paraphrases (e.g. "fully remote" -> "remote").
      * prompts.py   — System prompt and chat-formatted few-shot examples
                       (positive case, sponsorship case, header-only case).
      * client.py    — Async Qwen client over the DashScope OpenAI-compatible
                       endpoint with tenacity retries and a base-URL validator.
      * batch.py     — BigQuery-driven batch processor with bounded asyncio
                       concurrency and idempotent MERGE upserts keyed on
                       (job_id, snapshot_date) into staging.adzuna_jobs_llm_extract.
      * judge.py     — LLM-as-judge framework using Qwen-Plus to score each
                       field of the extractor's output as correct, incorrect,
                       or uncertain with one-line reasoning.
  - Added scripts/extract_llm_signals.py (Typer CLI) and
    scripts/evaluate_extraction.py (Typer CLI) as user-facing entry points,
    registered as ingest-llm-signals and evaluate-extraction console scripts
    in pyproject.toml.
  - Provisioned dbt intermediate layer:
      * models/intermediate/sources.yml exposing the LLM extract table to dbt.
      * models/intermediate/int_jobs_enriched.sql materialising a partitioned,
        clustered table that LEFT JOINs cleansed staging jobs with extraction
        outputs and adds two derived columns (has_extraction, skills_count).
      * models/intermediate/schema.yml documenting nine columns with seven
        column-level tests and one model-level uniqueness test.
      * tests/extraction_coverage_at_least_90pct.sql singular test
        enforcing the operational guarantee that at least 90 percent of
        rows carry an extraction.
  - Updated .env.example with the optional DASHSCOPE_BASE_URL override and
    JUDGE_MODEL alias for the evaluator.

Verifications passed
  - Single-row smoke run reached the DashScope endpoint and persisted to
    BigQuery after the credential plumbing was repaired.
  - 100-row sample run (concurrency 5) completed in approximately one
    minute and seeded the evaluation set.
  - LLM-as-judge evaluation produced an iterative quality-improvement
    sequence: 90.8 percent overall accuracy at baseline, 88.2 percent
    after a constraint-tightening revision that surfaced a few-shot
    anchor regression on duplicate template postings, then 95.9 percent
    after introducing a third "header-only / defaults" few-shot example.
    Per-field accuracy of the final prompt was required_skills 94.1,
    years_experience 100.0, sponsorship_signal 100.0,
    local_experience_required 100.0, remote_friendly 85.3.
  - Track-C full run extracted 3,925 of 3,925 pending rows
    (3,922 ok, 3 enum-validation failures) at the first pass; after
    adding pre-validators the three failures resolved on a one-row
    re-run, leaving 3,975 ok rows total in
    staging.adzuna_jobs_llm_extract.
  - dbt build over the intermediate layer materialised int_jobs_enriched
    and ran 11 tests with PASS=11 / WARN=0 / ERROR=0, including the
    custom coverage test which reported 100.0 percent enrichment.
  - Diagnostic SQL on int_jobs_enriched confirmed 3,975 total rows, 100
    percent extraction coverage, two explicit_yes sponsorship signals,
    seventy-six fully-remote roles, and an average of 0.42 extracted
    skills per job.

Known limitations recorded during validation
  - Adzuna search-API descriptions are truncated at approximately 500
    characters; the average extracted-skill count of 0.42 per job
    quantifies the impact (roughly 90 percent of JDs do not reach a
    skills section within the truncation window). Skill-level analysis
    in the marts layer should be qualified with this constraint.
  - Adzuna salary fields remain model-predicted rather than parsed from
    JD text; downstream salary analytics should filter by
    salary_is_predicted = 'verified' or rely on LLM-extracted salary
    once a future iteration enriches against the per-job /jobs/{id}
    endpoint.
  - The LLM judge is not infallible. Observed one false-positive case
    where the judge flagged "certification sponsorship" as a visa
    sponsorship signal; the extractor correctly returned unspecified.
    Future evaluation iterations could include explicit
    judge-calibration probes.
  - For the staffing-agency template JDs that re-post identical text
    across cities, residual skill hallucinations occasionally extract
    role descriptors ("Data Center", "infrastructure") rather than
    technologies. Documented as a data-quality limitation tied to the
    truncated-description issue above.

Defects resolved
  - DashScope authentication failed first against qwen-plus-2025-12-01,
    which requires paid activation in the user's tenant. Switched the
    default to qwen-turbo and exposed QWEN_MODEL plus DASHSCOPE_BASE_URL
    overrides in .env.example to support the international (Singapore)
    free-tier endpoint used by the user.
  - The DASHSCOPE_BASE_URL value initially included the variable name
    in its value field due to manual edit duplication, which produced
    httpx.UnsupportedProtocol errors that were opaque from the original
    raise_for_status path. Added an explicit base-URL validator that
    surfaces a descriptive ExtractionError before any HTTP request is
    issued.
  - Three full-run extractions failed Pydantic validation because the
    model emitted "fully remote" / "full remote" instead of the
    canonical enum value "remote". Added mode='before' field validators
    on remote_friendly and sponsorship_signal that map common synonyms
    to canonical enum values so downstream parsing succeeds without
    weakening the schema contract.
  - The first prompt-iteration round produced a regression in
    required_skills accuracy (76.0 -> 70.6 percent) because vague
    template descriptions caused the model to copy skill lists from the
    second few-shot example. Resolved by adding a third "header-only
    description -> default outputs" few-shot example, raising
    required_skills accuracy to 94.1 percent.

    - adzuna_pipeline/extraction/schema.py

[2026-05-08]  v0.3.0 — Staging layer with cleansed views and 17 data tests
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Introduced dbt-utils 1.x as a project dependency via dbt/packages.yml
    to support unique_combination_of_columns and expression_is_true tests.
  - Authored three staging models materialised as views:
      * stg_adzuna__jobs.sql  — flattens nested location/company/category
        records, casts ISO timestamps, strips HTML tags from descriptions,
        derives a salary_avg column, and deduplicates the row set on
        (id, snapshot_date) using QUALIFY ROW_NUMBER() to remove cross-
        city query overlap.
      * stg_occupation_lists.sql — normalises list_name casing and adds
        boolean membership flags (is_mltssl / is_stsol / is_csol).
      * stg_eoi.sql — derives round_year and round_month columns and
        normalises visa_subclass casing.
  - Documented the three models and their key columns in
    dbt/models/staging/schema.yml and added 17 data tests covering
    grain uniqueness, NOT NULL invariants, accepted-value enumerations,
    and non-negative numeric ranges.
  - The state accepted_values test runs at WARN severity to surface
    legitimate-but-unenumerated states (ACT, NT, etc.) without blocking
    builds.

Verifications passed
  - dbt deps installed dbt-utils without conflicts.
  - dbt run --select staging materialised three views in 4 seconds.
  - dbt test --select staging executed 17 tests with PASS=17 / WARN=0 /
    ERROR=0 in 11.7 seconds, including the cross-city deduplication
    invariant on (job_id, snapshot_date).
  - Sampling stg_adzuna__jobs returned cleansed records with normalised
    state (Queensland, New South Wales) and city (Brisbane Region,
    Sydney Region) columns and a populated salary_avg.
  - stg_occupation_lists exposed all 10 reference rows with correct
    boolean flags. stg_eoi exposed all 8 rounds in reverse chronological
    order.

Known limitations discovered during validation
  - Adzuna salary_min / salary_max are produced by Adzuna's predictive
    model rather than parsed from the job description. Observed
    discrepancies of more than fifty percent between salary_avg and the
    salary range stated explicitly in the job title. Filter by
    salary_is_predicted = 'verified' before using these fields for
    statistical analysis, or extract salary from description via the
    LLM extraction stage.
  - Adzuna location.area[2] returns region-level identifiers
    ('Melbourne Region', 'Brisbane Region') rather than city-proper
    names. This is sufficient for state-level and metropolitan-area
    analysis. Use location.area[3] for suburb-level granularity.

Defects resolved
  - The original (job_id, snapshot_date) uniqueness test failed with
    711 duplicate rows. Investigation showed that the same Adzuna job
    posting commonly surfaces in more than one per-city ingestion run,
    typically for remote or national roles. Resolved in
    stg_adzuna__jobs.sql by deduplicating on (id, snapshot_date) using
    QUALIFY ROW_NUMBER() OVER (... ORDER BY source_city) = 1, retaining
    the alphabetically-first source_city for determinism. The
    authoritative geographic attribution is taken from location.area
    rather than source_city.
  - dbt 1.11 emitted seven MissingArgumentsPropertyInGenericTestDeprecation
    warnings because generic-test parameters were declared at the test's
    top level. Migrated all generic-test argument blocks (accepted_values,
    expression_is_true, unique_combination_of_columns) to the new nested
    `arguments:` form for compatibility with the dbt 2.0 contract.

Changes
  Added
    - dbt/packages.yml
    - dbt/models/staging/stg_adzuna__jobs.sql
    - dbt/models/staging/stg_occupation_lists.sql
    - dbt/models/staging/stg_eoi.sql
  Changed
    - dbt/models/staging/schema.yml: full rewrite with three documented
      models and 17 data tests using the dbt 1.11 nested-arguments form.
  Fixed
    - Cross-city duplicate rows in stg_adzuna__jobs.
    - dbt 1.11 generic-test argument deprecation across seven sites.

[2026-05-07]  v0.2.0 — Adzuna ingestion pipeline and raw-layer seeds
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Implemented the adzuna_pipeline ingestion package as three cooperating
    modules:
      * client.py   — httpx-based search-API client with tenacity-driven
                      exponential backoff, request-level rate limiting,
                      typed SearchQuery / SearchPage data classes, and a
                      pagination iterator.
      * storage.py  — Cloud Storage uploader that serialises records as
                      gzipped newline-delimited JSON under the canonical
                      key gs://{bucket}/adzuna/snapshot_date=YYYY-MM-DD/
                      {city}.jsonl.gz, injecting snapshot_date,
                      ingested_at, and source_city as observability
                      metadata on every record.
      * loader.py   — BigQuery loader exposing an explicit SchemaField
                      list for raw.adzuna_jobs (including nested location,
                      company, and category records), DAY partitioning on
                      snapshot_date, clustering on source_city, and
                      idempotent partition replacement.
  - Added scripts/ingest_adzuna.py as the end-to-end orchestrator with
    Typer-based CLI flags for cities, snapshot date, dry-run, partition
    replacement, and logging level. Emits a per-city summary table and a
    non-zero exit code when any city fails.
  - Provisioned dbt seeds for the raw layer:
      * dbt/seeds/occupation_lists.csv (10-row MLTSSL/STSOL sample)
      * dbt/seeds/eoi_invitations.csv (8-row 189/491 round sample)
      * dbt/seeds/README.md documenting upstream sources for production
        replacement.
  - Registered seed column types in dbt_project.yml under seeds.
  - Authored dbt/macros/generate_schema_name.sql to override dbt's
    default schema-naming convention so the model-level schema declaration
    is used verbatim.
  - Updated pyproject.toml with an ingest-adzuna console-script entry
    point and amended .gitignore to retain dbt/seeds/*.csv in version
    control.
  - Refactored adzuna_pipeline/__init__.py to re-export the public API
    surface (clients, schemas, data classes).

Verifications passed
  - Dry run with --max-pages 2 fetched 300 records across the three
    cities, uploaded three gzipped JSONL objects to GCS, and skipped the
    BigQuery load step as expected.
  - Production run with --replace-partition loaded 4,704 records into
    raw.adzuna_jobs (Sydney 2,806; Melbourne 1,102; Brisbane 796).
  - bq ls au-jobs-radar:raw confirmed three tables present, with
    adzuna_jobs reporting DAY partitioning on snapshot_date and clustering
    on source_city.
  - dbt seed loaded 10 rows into raw.occupation_lists and 8 rows into
    raw.eoi_invitations after the schema-name macro override.
  - Aggregate diagnostic query returned 4,704 jobs across 3 source cities
    with salary disclosure rates between 10.4 and 15.5 percent and average
    minimum salaries between A$121K and A$140K.

Known limitations
  - Adzuna search-API job descriptions are truncated at approximately 500
    characters; the per-city averages are 499.67, 500.00, and 499.90
    characters. Full descriptions require per-job calls to /jobs/{id}.
    Decision on whether to enrich a sample subset is deferred to the LLM
    extraction phase.

Defects resolved
  - Initial dry-run hit cascading HTTP 503 responses from Adzuna for
    Melbourne, exhausting the four-attempt retry budget. Raised
    RETRY_ATTEMPTS to 6 and RETRY_WAIT_MAX_SECONDS to 30, providing a
    cumulative wait budget of approximately 31 seconds per page.
  - httpx logged each request URL at INFO level, exposing the Adzuna
    app_id and app_key in console output. Suppressed httpx, httpcore,
    google.auth, and urllib3 loggers to WARNING in the orchestrator.
  - dbt seed initially materialised tables under a staging_raw dataset
    because the default generate_schema_name macro concatenates
    target.schema with the model-level +schema configuration. Overrode
    the macro so model-level schema declarations are honoured verbatim;
    dropped the misplaced staging_raw dataset and re-ran dbt seed with
    --full-refresh.
