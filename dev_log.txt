[2026-05-12]  v0.5.1 — Q1 demand proxy migrated from EOI to ANZSCO ceilings
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Replaced the EOI-based demand metric in fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d
    with the Home Affairs SkillSelect annual occupation ceiling (PY25/26) and
    prior programme-year grants (PY24/25), joined at the 4-digit ANZSCO unit
    group level.
  - Authored dbt/seeds/occupation_ceilings.csv (11 rows covering 10 ICT and
    cross-domain unit groups plus one placeholder row for 2632 ICT Support
    and Test Engineers, which is absent from the published ceiling list).
    Each row carries tier, avg_stock, annual_ceiling, grants_py24_25,
    remaining_py25_26, program_year, source, source_url, and retrieved_date.
  - Reworked fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d.sql:
      * Dropped the eoi_aggregates CTE and the LEFT JOIN to stg_eoi.
      * Added a ceilings CTE joining on substr(anzsco_code, 1, 4).
      * Renamed measures: eoi_invitations_recent / eoi_per_job_ratio are
        retired; the model now exposes annual_ceiling, grants_py24_25,
        remaining_py25_26, jobs_to_ceiling_ratio (jobs_count_30d divided by
        annual_ceiling) and grants_to_jobs_ratio (grants_py24_25 divided by
        jobs_count_30d).
      * Surfaced anzsco_unit_group, unit_group_name, and tier as new columns.
  - Updated dbt/dbt_project.yml to register occupation_ceilings with typed
    column definitions.
  - Updated dbt/models/marts/schema.yml: rewrote the model description and
    column documentation; added a not_null test on anzsco_unit_group; left
    annual_ceiling and grants_py24_25 nullable to accommodate unit groups
    not on the ceiling list.
  - Updated scripts/verify/day5_marts_check.py: fixed the legacy __TABLES__
    column reference (table_name -> table_id) and rewrote the Q1 query to
    select the new ceiling-based columns.

Rationale
  - The v0.5.0 EOI seed faithfully reflected the Home Affairs public EOI
    feed, which publishes invitations per round per visa subclass (189 /
    491) without an ANZSCO breakdown. The join in
    fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d consequently yielded zero demand for
    every occupation, breaking the Q1 supply-versus-demand narrative.
  - The PY25/26 occupation ceiling (released under FOI FA 26/01/00545 and
    republished by Delta Immigration) is the most defensible public proxy
    for invitation competition at the occupation level. Combining it with
    PY24/25 grants exposes the over-allocation pattern in tier-4 ICT unit
    groups (for example 2613 Software and Applications Programmers issued
    3,800 grants against a 912 ceiling).
  - stg_eoi and raw.eoi_invitations remain in the project for dashboard-
    level aggregate KPIs (total 189 invitations across recent rounds) and
    are no longer joined to the per-occupation fact.

Verifications passed
  - dbt build over the full project (4 seeds, 9 models, 50 data tests)
    completed in 43.5 seconds with PASS=63 / WARN=0 / ERROR=0.
  - day5_marts_check.py confirmed non-zero ceiling and grants for nine of
    ten occupations; 263213 ICT Quality Assurance Engineer correctly
    surfaces NULL because unit group 2632 is absent from the ceiling list.
  - Sample insights validated against the new measures:
      * 261313 Software Engineer: 455 jobs / 912 ceiling = 0.499 jobs per
        ceiling slot; 3,800 grants in PY24/25 against 455 advertised jobs
        gives a grants_to_jobs ratio of 8.35.
      * 261111 ICT Business Analyst: 217 jobs / 500 ceiling = 0.434;
        2,018 grants gives a grants_to_jobs ratio of 9.30.
      * 263213 ICT Quality Assurance Engineer: 7 jobs with no published
        ceiling, exposing the "MLTSSL dead end" case for the LinkedIn
        narrative.

Known limitations
  - The ceiling join collapses 6-digit ANZSCO codes within the same 4-digit
    unit group onto a single ceiling value (for example 261311, 261312,
    261313, and 261314 all share the 2613 ceiling of 912). This is
    intrinsic to how Home Affairs publishes the cap and is documented in
    the README and in the model description.
  - The ceilings seed sources from a third-party republication of the
    Home Affairs FOI release because the original Home Affairs occupation
    ceiling page was not reachable at the time of authoring. The Home
    Affairs FOI reference (FA 26/01/00545) is recorded in the seed.

Changes
  Added
    - dbt/seeds/occupation_ceilings.csv
  Changed
    - dbt/dbt_project.yml: registered occupation_ceilings column types.
    - dbt/models/marts/fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d.sql: ceiling-based
      demand join replaces EOI join.
    - dbt/models/marts/schema.yml: rewrote model description and column
      documentation; added not_null test on anzsco_unit_group.
    - scripts/verify/day5_marts_check.py: __TABLES__ column fix and Q1
      column rewrite.
  Retired (logical, files retained)
    - stg_eoi and raw.eoi_invitations are no longer consumed by Q1; they
      remain in the project for aggregate-KPI use.

[2026-05-10]  v0.5.0 — Marts layer with conformed dimension and three fact tables
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Added a third dbt seed, anzsco_title_patterns.csv (14 rows of
    priority / anzsco_code / occupation_name / title_pattern), to bridge
    the gap between Adzuna's free-text job titles and the structured
    ANZSCO codes consumed by the EOI dataset.
  - Authored an additional intermediate model,
    int_jobs_anzsco_mapped.sql, which performs a regex-driven left join
    between job titles and the title-pattern seed and resolves multi-
    pattern matches via QUALIFY ROW_NUMBER ordered by priority.
  - Authored the marts layer:
      * dim_occupation.sql        — conformed occupation dimension keyed
                                    on anzsco_code, unioning codes from
                                    the migration lists and the title-
                                    pattern seed and surfacing list-
                                    membership flags.
      * fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d.sql
                                  — Q1 fact. Jobs (supply) and EOI
                                    invitations (demand) per ANZSCO,
                                    plus the eoi_per_job_ratio measure.
      * fct_local_experience_barrier.sql
                                  — Q2 fact. (anzsco_code, state) grain;
                                    local_experience_pct and
                                    sponsorship_yes_pct measures with a
                                    minimum-cell-size guard at five
                                    postings.
      * fct_skills_demand.sql     — Q3 supporting fact. Per-(state,
                                    skill) mention counts unnested from
                                    the LLM required_skills array,
                                    filtered to a minimum of three
                                    mentions per state.
  - Added column documentation and 14 new tests in
    models/marts/schema.yml (uniqueness, not-null, relationships to
    dim_occupation, ratio range checks, composite key uniqueness).
  - Updated dbt_project.yml seed configuration to register column
    types for the new title-pattern seed, and updated
    models/intermediate/schema.yml to document the new
    int_jobs_anzsco_mapped model.

Verifications passed
  - dbt build over the full project (3 seeds, 9 models, 51 tests,
    4 sources, 656 macros) completed in 42.2 seconds with PASS=63 /
    WARN=0 / ERROR=0.
  - dbt docs generate produced catalog.json without functional errors;
    the agate "table_owner" RuntimeWarning is a known cosmetic message
    in dbt-bigquery 1.11 with no operational impact.
  - Sampled marts data exercised all three fact tables successfully:
      * fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d returned per-ANZSCO supply
        and demand rows including derived eoi_per_job_ratio.
      * fct_local_experience_barrier returned (anzsco_code, state)
        rows with local_experience_pct and sponsorship_yes_pct in the
        valid 0..1 range.
      * fct_skills_demand returned per-state skill mention counts
        ordered by demand.

Known limitations
  - Title-pattern coverage is approximately seventy to eighty percent
    of IT-class jobs. Postings whose titles do not match any pattern
    carry a NULL anzsco_code and are excluded from per-occupation
    aggregates; their volume is implicit in the difference between
    int_jobs_enriched row count and the sum of jobs_count_30d across
    fct_occupation_supply_demand_30d.
  - Several common AU IT job titles map to a single ANZSCO code only
    by approximation. For example data-scientist roles are attributed
    to 261313 Software Engineer because no dedicated ANZSCO code
    exists. Documented in the title-pattern seed.
  - The fct_local_experience_barrier minimum-cell-size guard
    (total_jobs >= 5) drops sparse (occupation, state) combinations
    such as ICT Quality Assurance Engineer in Tasmania. This trades
    coverage for ratio stability and is appropriate for a v1 dashboard.

[2026-05-15]  v0.7.0 — Single-page Visa Applicant View dashboard
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Scope of work
  - Built the Power BI data model on the four marts tables: hidden
    technical key columns, applied percentage and currency formats,
    and authored five DAX measures (Total Jobs 30d, Occupations
    Covered, MLTSSL Occupations, Local Experience Rate, Sponsorship
    Offered Rate).
  - Produced a three-column single-page layout: a vertical KPI stack
    on the left, a centre column with the Q2 local-experience bar
    chart over the Q1 supply bar chart, and a right column with the
    Q3 skills bar chart over the Q1 four-quadrant scatter. A small
    filled map sits beneath the KPIs.
  - Each of the six visuals carries an insight-style title rather
    than a field-name title.
  - The Q1 scatter applies jobs_count_30d >= 20 as a visual filter to
    suppress noise occupations and a log-scale Y axis to surface
    points that would otherwise compress to the X axis under the
    skew of one outlier occupation. Median X and Y constant lines
    divide the plot into the four quadrants.
  - Authored docs/powerbi-dashboard-guide.md as the build manual for
    the dashboard.

Verifications passed
  - The KPI row reads 1,010 mapped IT roles, 10 occupations with
    demand, and 9 MLTSSL occupations tracked.
  - The Q1 supply chart ranks Software Engineer (455), ICT Business
    Analyst (217), Computer Network and Systems Engineer (128), and
    ICT Security Specialist (104) in descending order.
  - The Q2 barrier chart ranks Software Tester, Computer Network, and
    ICT Security Specialist as the top three; Software Engineer
    appears with the lowest rate among the populated occupations.
  - The Q3 skills chart ranks AWS, Azure, and Salesforce as the top
    three, an order consistent with the AU IT market favouring
    platform skills over raw programming languages.

Defects resolved
  - The Day 6 "Avg Local Experience Rate" measure used a simple
    AVERAGEX over the per-(occupation, state) percentages and therefore
    ignored bucket weighting (Simpson's paradox). Replaced with
    DIVIDE(SUM(local_experience_count), SUM(total_jobs)), which returns
    the correctly weighted rate at any aggregation grain.
  - The first version of the Q1 scatter compressed all points against
    the X axis because one occupation with jobs_count_30d in single
    digits produced a grants_to_jobs_ratio in the hundreds. Resolved
    with a visual filter (jobs_count_30d >= 20) and a Y-axis log scale.
  - Iterated through three layout options before settling on the
    final three-column grid. Earlier vertical-KPIs-with-stacked-bars
    arrangements suffered from imbalanced visual weight; the eventual
    three-column grid with a small corner map gives every visual
    comparable canvas area while keeping the geography element
    de-emphasised.

Known limitations
  - The title-pattern dictionary maps approximately 1,010 of the
    3,975 enriched postings to ANZSCO codes; the remaining 2,965 are
    excluded from per-occupation aggregations.
  - Local Experience Rate is approximately one percent in absolute
    terms; the headline insight is the relative ordering across
    states and occupations, not the absolute level. The Methodology
    page (v0.8.0) documents this.

Changes
  Added
    - docs/powerbi-dashboard-guide.md
  Changed
    - Power BI .pbix (local, gitignored): data model built, five
      measures authored, six visuals laid out on a single page.
